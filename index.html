Interesting facts about Chameleon
  • Chameleons vary in size. Chameleons prefer life in the forest and they adapted to the arboreal locomotion (movement in the trees). Chameleons also use their long tail when moving in the trees to grab a branch and secure their position.
  • A Chameleon is a lizard like reptile of the genus Chamaeleo, of several. species, found in Africa, Asia, and Europe.

Interesting facts about Camel
  • With the exception of rutting males competing for females, camels are very peaceful animals who rarely exhibit aggression. Contrary to popular misconception, camels do not store water in their humps.
  • Dromedary Camels live in the Middle East, North Africa, West Asia and in the Australian Outback.
  • Camels can reach 7 feet in height (at the hump) and weigh up to 1500 pounds. Camel's ears are furry. They are specially adapted to the life in desert.
